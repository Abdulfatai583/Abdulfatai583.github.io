Project Overview

Cardiovascular diseases are among the leading causes of death globally. This project analyzes patient health data to identify key risk factors associated with heart attack occurrence and support data-driven preventive healthcare decisions.

The objective is to help healthcare professionals, analysts, and policymakers quickly identify high-risk populations and understand which health and lifestyle variables contribute most to heart attack risk.

Dataset Summary

The dataset contains over 158,000 patient records with demographic, medical, and lifestyle information, including:

  • Age and gender
  • Income level
  • Cholesterol and blood pressure levels
  • Diabetes and hypertension status
  • Sleep duration and stress levels
  • Physical activity patterns

Data Preparation

  • Handled missing and inconsistent values using Power Query
  • Grouped continuous variables such as age and income into analytical categories
  • Created derived features including cholesterol risk groups and heart attack indicators
  • Built a structured data model optimized for Power BI analysis

Key Findings & Insights

Overall Metrics

  • Total Patients Analyzed: 158,000
  • Heart Attack Rate: ~40%
  • Average Sleep Duration: 6.48 hours
  • Average Cholesterol Level: 199.53

Demographic & Regional Insights

  • Urban regions recorded significantly more heart attack cases than rural areas, suggesting higher lifestyle-related risk exposure.
  • Middle-aged adults showed the highest number of heart attack cases, followed by older age groups.
  • Males and individuals within lower-income categories exhibited slightly higher risk levels.

Health & Lifestyle Insights

  • Patients with hypertension and diabetes showed a notably higher likelihood of heart attacks.
  • High stress levels and insufficient sleep correlated strongly with increased risk.
  • Moderate physical activity and healthy sleep patterns were associated with lower risk rates.

Recommendations

  1. Encourage regular physical activity and stress-management programs, especially in urban areas.
  2. Promote early health screenings for individuals with high cholesterol, diabetes, or hypertension.
  3. Increase public awareness around adequate sleep (6â€“8 hours) and balanced nutrition.
  4. Implement targeted interventions for middle-aged and low-income populations.

Tools & Technologies

  • Power BI Desktop: Data modeling, DAX measures, and dashboard development
  • Power Query: Data cleaning and transformation
  • Excel: Initial data inspection and validation

Conclusion

This project demonstrates how lifestyle behaviors and underlying medical conditions significantly influence heart attack risk. By leveraging Power BI for analysis and visualization, the dashboard delivers actionable insights that support preventive healthcare planning.

The analysis highlights the importance of data-driven decision-making in reducing the long-term burden of cardiovascular disease.
